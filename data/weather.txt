NIGERIA AGRO-ECOLOGICAL ZONES

Nigeria has 6 major agro-ecological zones, each with distinct rainfall, temperature, and farming calendars.

---

SUDAN SAVANNA ZONE
States: Sokoto, Kebbi, Zamfara, Katsina, Kano, Jigawa, Borno, Yobe, northern Bauchi, northern Gombe
Annual rainfall: 500-900mm
Rainy season: June-September (4-5 months only)
Dry season: October-May (7-8 months)
Temperature: 15-45 degrees C (very hot in April-May, cool December-January)
Dominant winds: Harmattan (dry, dusty NE wind) November-February; Southwest monsoon June-September
Farming calendar:
- April-May: Land preparation. Plant early millet and cowpea before rains.
- June: First rains - plant maize, sorghum, groundnut, cowpea, sesame
- July-August: Weeding season. Apply fertilizer top-dress.
- September: Late rains. Harvest early varieties.
- October-November: Main harvest season. Groundnut, sorghum, cowpea.
- November-March: Fadama dry season farming. Tomato, onion, garlic, wheat, pepper.
Best crops: Millet, sorghum, groundnut, cowpea, sesame, onion, tomato (irrigated), wheat, hibiscus
Water harvesting: Critical in this zone. Use half-moon water harvesting pits. Zai pits for degraded land.
Drought risk: High. Plant early-maturing drought-tolerant varieties. SAMMAZ 27 for maize, Kanannado for cowpea.
Investor tip: Lowest land cost in Nigeria. Best for irrigated dry season vegetables and export crops (sesame, hibiscus).

---

GUINEA SAVANNA ZONE (NORTH)
States: Niger, Kwara, Kogi, Nasarawa, southern Kaduna, southern Bauchi, Gombe, Adamawa, Taraba, Benue, Plateau
Annual rainfall: 900-1400mm
Rainy season: April-October (6-7 months)
Dry season: November-March (4-5 months)
Temperature: 18-38 degrees C
Farming calendar:
- March-April: Land preparation begins. Early planting of maize and cassava.
- April-May: Main planting season. Maize, sorghum, cowpea, soybean, cassava, yam.
- June-August: Weeding. Fertilizer application. Second planting of cowpea.
- September-October: Harvesting early crops. Maize, cowpea.
- October-November: Cassava, yam harvest begins. Continues to March.
- November-February: Dry season. Some areas have fadama farming.
Best crops: Maize, sorghum, cowpea, soybean, cassava, yam, ginger, sesame, rice, mango
Soil: Mix of sandy loam and clay. Generally good fertility.
Investor tip: Middle Belt is Nigeria's food basket. Best zone for large-scale maize, soybean, rice, and cassava investment.

---

DERIVED GUINEA SAVANNA / FOREST-SAVANNA TRANSITION
States: Oyo, Ogun, Osun, Ekiti, Kwara south, Ondo north, Kogi south, Anambra, Enugu, Imo, Abia, Ebonyi
Annual rainfall: 1200-1500mm
Rainy season: Two seasons. March-July (first), September-November (second)
Dry season: December-February
Temperature: 21-35 degrees C
Farming calendar:
- February-March: First season land preparation. Plant maize, vegetables, cowpea.
- March-July: First cropping season. Maize, cassava, yam, vegetables.
- July-August: Short dry spell. Harvest first season crops.
- August-September: Second season planting. Maize, cowpea, vegetables, cassava (ratoon).
- September-November: Second season crops grow. Heavy rainfall.
- November-January: Harvest second season. Dry season begins.
Best crops: Cassava, yam, maize, cocoa, oil palm, citrus, plantain, vegetables
Investor tip: Two farming seasons per year doubles investment returns. Good for cassava processing, cocoa, oil palm.

---

HUMID FOREST / RAINFOREST ZONE
States: Cross River, Akwa Ibom, Rivers, Bayelsa, Delta, Edo, Lagos, Ondo south, Ogun south, Abia, Imo
Annual rainfall: 1500-3000mm (highest in Nigeria)
Rainy season: Almost year-round (10-11 months)
Dry season: December-February (very short)
Temperature: 23-32 degrees C year-round
Farming calendar:
- January-February: Short dry spell. Land clearing. Plant cassava, yam.
- March-May: Heavy rains begin. Plant vegetables, plantain, banana, cocoa.
- June-September: Peak rains. Weeding critical. Monitor for fungal diseases.
- October-November: Moderate rains. Harvest season for most crops.
- December: Short dry season. Harvest root crops.
Best crops: Cassava, yam, plantain, banana, cocoa, rubber, oil palm, coconut, pineapple, vegetables
Disease pressure: High humidity means high fungal disease pressure. Spray more frequently.
Investor tip: Best for perennial tree crops. High humidity and rainfall means low irrigation cost but high disease management cost.

---

MONTANE / HIGHLAND ZONE
States: Plateau (Jos Plateau), Taraba (Mambilla Plateau), Adamawa highlands, Bauchi highlands
Annual rainfall: 1200-1600mm
Rainy season: April-October
Temperature: 10-28 degrees C (cool at altitude - Jos averages 18-22 degrees C year-round)
Special features: Cool temperatures allow crops impossible elsewhere in Nigeria: strawberry, apple, temperate vegetables, wheat, irish potato
Farming calendar:
- February-March: Land preparation. Irish potato planting begins.
- April-May: Main planting season. Maize, vegetables, potato, tomato.
- June-August: Peak growing season. Cool temperatures favor quality vegetables.
- September-October: Harvest season. Potato, tomato, cabbage, carrot.
- November-January: Second season or dry season preparation.
Best crops: Irish potato, tomato, cabbage, carrot, lettuce, strawberry, apple, wheat, maize, ginger
Investor tip: Unique cool-climate zone in Nigeria. Premium vegetables command higher prices. Jos Irish potato is best in Nigeria.

---

SAHEL SAVANNA ZONE
States: Extreme north of Borno, Yobe, Sokoto, Kebbi (Lake Chad basin area)
Annual rainfall: 250-500mm (very low)
Rainy season: July-August (2-3 months only)
Dry season: September-June (9-10 months)
Temperature: 15-48 degrees C (extreme heat April-June)
Farming calendar:
- July: Plant drought-tolerant crops immediately at first rains. Early millet (90 days), cowpea.
- August: Weed and fertilize quickly. Rain ends fast.
- September: Harvest. Everything must be done before October.
- October-June: Very long dry season. Irrigated farming near Lake Chad possible.
Best crops: Early millet (Gero), cowpea, groundnut (very short season varieties only)
Special notes: Climate change has made this zone increasingly unreliable. Rainfall starting later and ending earlier.
Investor warning: High climate risk. Only invest with guaranteed water source (Lake Chad, borehole, irrigation).

MONTHLY FARMING CALENDAR - NIGERIA (GENERAL GUIDE)

JANUARY
- North: Dry season fadama farming peak. Harvest onion, garlic, tomato, wheat.
- South: Short dry season. Harvest yam, cassava from October plantings. Land clearing.
- Weather: Harmattan winds (dusty, dry, cold mornings hot afternoons). Visibility poor.
- Investor action: Market prices high for vegetables in south. Good time to sell stored grain.

FEBRUARY
- North: Fadama harvest continues. Begin land preparation for rainy season.
- South: Land preparation begins. Plant cassava cuttings in southern states.
- Weather: Hot and dry. Temperature rising. Harmattan weakening.
- Farming tip: Treat seeds with fungicide before storing. Check store for pests.

MARCH
- North: Continue land prep. Plant early maize in far south of Sudan savanna.
- South: First rains expected in south (Lagos, Ogun, Cross River). Plant maize, vegetables.
- Weather: First rains may arrive south. North remains dry. Temperature peaks.
- Investor action: Input prices rise as planting season approaches. Buy inputs early.

APRIL
- North: First rains may arrive in southern Guinea savanna. Plant cowpea, groundnut.
- South: Full planting season. Maize, cassava, yam, vegetables.
- Planting warning: Do not plant before rains are established - wait for 2-3 consecutive rainfalls.
- Pest alert: Termites active before rains. Treat planting holes.

MAY
- North: Rains established in Guinea savanna. Main planting season. Maize, sorghum, cowpea.
- South: Active growing season. Fertilize maize at knee-high stage.
- Weather: Thunderstorms and heavy rain possible. Lightning risk.
- Farmer tip: Apply urea top-dress when rain is forecast not after.

JUNE
- North: Sudan savanna rains begin. Plant millet, sorghum, groundnut, cowpea.
- South: Heavy rains. Watch for flooding in low areas. Waterlogging risk.
- Pest alert: Stem borer moths active. Scout maize fields for egg masses.
- Disease alert: Fungal diseases increase with humidity. Apply preventive fungicide to tomato.

JULY
- North: Full rainy season. Apply fertilizer to all crops. Weed urgently.
- South: Mid-season. First season maize nearing harvest. Second season preparation.
- Flooding risk: Niger, Benue, Anambra river basins. Monitor water levels.
- Farmer tip: Critical weeding month. Weeds between rows compete most at this stage.

AUGUST
- North: Heavy rains. Crops at flowering and grain fill. Very critical month.
- South: Short dry spell may occur (August break). Stress for crops. Irrigate if possible.
- Pest alert: Fall armyworm attacks maize. Check for caterpillars in whorl.
- Investor action: Cassava prices often lowest now. Good time to buy and process into garri.

SEPTEMBER
- North: Early harvest begins. Cowpea, early maize. Groundnut pegging complete.
- South: Second season planting begins. Heavy rains return after August break.
- Harvest tip: Monitor grain moisture. Do not harvest wet grain.
- Weather: Storm season peak. Lodging risk for tall crops.

OCTOBER
- North: Main harvest month. Sorghum, groundnut, cowpea, maize. Busy month.
- South: First season harvest continues. Yam, cassava, cocoa pods ripening.
- Storage alert: Aflatoxin risk in groundnut during drying. Dry quickly below 9% moisture.
- Market tip: Grain prices at annual low. Consider storing until January-March.

NOVEMBER
- North: Harvest largely complete. Fadama planting begins. Tomato, onion, pepper.
- South: Harvest continues. Cocoa fermentation and drying.
- Weather: Harmattan begins. Good drying weather for grain and cocoa.
- Investor action: Best month to buy grain cheaply for storage.

DECEMBER
- North: Fadama crops growing. Irrigate every 4-5 days.
- South: Short dry season. Cassava, yam harvest. Christmas market demand high.
- Market: Staple food prices start rising as stocks deplete. Good for farmers with storage.
- Planning: Order seeds and fertilizers early for next season.

WEATHER RISKS AND MANAGEMENT

DROUGHT
Risk areas: Sudan savanna, Sahel zone, northern Nigeria generally
Signs: Rainfall below 50mm per month during growing season, plants wilting by mid-morning
Management:
- Plant drought-tolerant varieties (see crop-specific recommendations)
- Use zai pits or half-moon water harvesting structures
- Mulch heavily to conserve soil moisture
- Irrigate when soil is dry 5cm below surface
- Plant shorter-season varieties that mature before dry spell

FLOODING
Risk areas: Niger-Benue confluence (Lokoja), Cross River basin, Niger Delta, coastal states, Kogi
Flood-prone months: August-October (peak flooding)
Management:
- Plant flood-tolerant varieties (FARO 52 rice, flood-tolerant cassava)
- Build bunds around fields
- Have drainage channels ready before rains
- Monitor NIMET flood early warning alerts
- Never plant in lowest-lying fadama during main rains

HARMATTAN
Period: November-February
Risk: Reduces crop yield in vegetables, causes leaf scorching, dries out soil
Management:
- Irrigate more frequently during harmattan
- Use windbreaks (rows of trees) to reduce wind damage
- Harvest dry season crops before harmattan intensity peaks (before February)
- Store harvested grain with hermetic bags to prevent drying and pests

HEATWAVES
Risk areas: North Nigeria April-June before rains
Temperature can reach 45-48 degrees C in Sokoto, Kebbi
Management:
- Plant early or wait until rains establish - avoid planting into extreme heat
- Use shade nets for seedling nurseries in April-May
- Irrigate during early morning or evening only
