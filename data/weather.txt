NIGERIA WEATHER ZONES AND FARMING CALENDAR

SAHEL ZONE (Sokoto, Kebbi, Zamfara, Katsina, northern Kano, northern Borno)
Annual rainfall: 250-500mm
Rainy season: June-September (3-4 months only)
Dry season: October-May (very long, hot and dry)
Temperature: 25-40°C, can exceed 45°C in April
Harmattan: November-February, strong dusty winds from Sahara
Recommended crops: Millet, sorghum, cowpea, groundnut
Farming advice: Plant immediately rains start in June, use drought-tolerant varieties
Irrigation need: Essential for any dry season farming
Risk: Drought, locust invasion, desert encroachment
Flood risk: Low but flash floods possible in July-August

SUDAN SAVANNA ZONE (Sokoto south, Kano, Kaduna north, Jigawa, Yobe, Borno south)
Annual rainfall: 500-1000mm
Rainy season: May-October (5-6 months)
Dry season: November-April
Temperature: 22-38°C
Harmattan: December-February, moderate intensity
Recommended crops: Sorghum, millet, cowpea, groundnut, rice, maize, onion, tomato
Farming advice: Two crops possible in good rainfall years
Irrigation need: Needed for dry season vegetables
Risk: Erratic rainfall, meningitis season December-April
Flood risk: Medium, low-lying areas flood in August-September

GUINEA SAVANNA ZONE (Kaduna south, Niger, Kwara, Kogi, Benue, Plateau, Nassarawa, southern Kebbi)
Annual rainfall: 1000-1500mm
Rainy season: April-October (7 months)
Dry season: November-March
Temperature: 20-35°C
Harmattan: December-January, mild
Recommended crops: Maize, sorghum, yam, cassava, rice, cowpea, soybean, cotton
Farming advice: Best zone for maize and yam production
Irrigation need: Minimal, rainfed farming mostly reliable
Risk: Flooding in river basins, army worms in maize
Flood risk: High near River Niger and River Benue

DERIVED SAVANNA ZONE (Oyo, Ogun, Osun, Ondo, Edo, Delta north, Enugu, Anambra, Imo north)
Annual rainfall: 1200-1600mm
Rainy season: Two seasons — March-July and September-November
Dry season: December-February and August (short dry spell)
Temperature: 24-33°C
Harmattan: December-January, very mild
Recommended crops: Cassava, maize, yam, cocoa, plantain, vegetables
Farming advice: Plant first season crops in March, second season in September
Irrigation need: Usually not needed, rainfall well distributed
Risk: Soil erosion on slopes, flooding in low areas
Flood risk: Medium, especially September-October

RAINFOREST ZONE (Lagos, Ogun south, Ondo south, Delta south, Rivers, Bayelsa, Cross River, Akwa Ibom)
Annual rainfall: 1500-4000mm
Rainy season: Almost year-round, peaks March-July and September-November
Dry season: December-February only (short)
Temperature: 25-32°C, high humidity year-round
Harmattan: Very mild, barely noticeable
Recommended crops: Cassava, plantain, cocoyam, oil palm, rubber, vegetables
Farming advice: Can farm almost year-round but manage waterlogging
Irrigation need: Not needed, excess water management more important
Risk: Flooding, soil waterlogging, fungal diseases due to high humidity
Flood risk: Very high, especially coastal areas

HIGHLAND ZONE (Plateau state, Mambilla Plateau in Taraba, Obudu in Cross River)
Annual rainfall: 1000-1500mm
Rainy season: April-October
Dry season: November-March
Temperature: 12-28°C (much cooler than rest of Nigeria)
Harmattan: Moderate December-February
Recommended crops: Irish potato, wheat, barley, temperate vegetables, tea, coffee
Farming advice: Only zone suitable for Irish potato and temperate crops
Frost risk: Possible on Mambilla Plateau December-January at night
Irrigation need: Minimal
Risk: Frost damage, soil erosion on steep slopes
Flood risk: Low but erosion is serious concern

NIGERIA NATIONAL FARMING CALENDAR

JANUARY
- Harvest dry season tomato, pepper, onion in north
- Land preparation for early maize in south
- Irrigate vegetables in north
- Prune cocoa and citrus in south

FEBRUARY
- Plant yam in Guinea savanna and derived savanna
- Continue dry season vegetable harvest
- Apply fertilizer to irrigated crops
- Land clearing for first season planting

MARCH
- First rains arrive in south (Lagos, Ibadan, Benin)
- Plant maize, cassava, vegetables in south
- Nursery preparation for tomato in north
- Monitor for meningitis season in north

APRIL
- Rains spread to middle belt
- Plant maize in Benue, Kogi, Niger, Kwara
- Transplant tomato seedlings in Plateau
- Apply pre-emergent herbicide to fields

MAY
- Rains reach Guinea savanna zone
- Plant groundnut, cowpea, sorghum in Kaduna, Kano
- Second planting of maize in south
- Watch for army worm outbreak

JUNE
- Rains reach Sudan savanna and Sahel
- Plant millet, sorghum in far north
- Apply NPK fertilizer to early planted maize
- Watch for flooding in low-lying areas

JULY
- Peak rainy season across most of Nigeria
- Top dress maize and sorghum with urea
- Weed cassava and yam fields
- High flood risk near rivers

AUGUST
- Short dry spell in south (August break)
- Second season planting in south
- Harvest early maize in north
- Control striga weed in sorghum fields

SEPTEMBER
- Second rains in south
- Harvest maize in Guinea savanna
- Plant second season tomato in south
- Monitor for late blight in wet conditions

OCTOBER
- Main harvest season begins across Nigeria
- Harvest sorghum, millet, cowpea, groundnut
- Plant dry season vegetables in north (with irrigation)
- Store grains properly to avoid aflatoxin

NOVEMBER
- Major harvest across all zones
- Plant onion, tomato, pepper in Kano, Kebbi, Sokoto
- Dry and store harvested grains
- Prices lowest due to abundant supply

DECEMBER
- Post-harvest storage and marketing
- Harmattan begins — good for drying crops
- Plan next season — order seeds and fertilizer early
- Prices begin rising as supply moves to market
