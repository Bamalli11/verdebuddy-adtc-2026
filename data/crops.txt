MAIZE (CORN) - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kaduna, Kano, Plateau, Benue, Niger
Planting season: March-April (first season), July-August (second season)
Soil type: Well-drained loamy soil, pH 5.5-7.0
Maturity period: 90-120 days depending on variety
Common diseases: Maize streak virus, Northern leaf blight, Gray leaf spot
Pest management: Use carbofuran for stem borers, neem extract for aphids
Recommended varieties: SAMMAZ 15, SAMMAZ 27, OBA SUPER 2, TZPB SR
Fertilizer: Apply NPK 15-15-15 at planting, top dress with urea at 6 weeks
Yield potential: 3-5 tonnes per hectare with good management
Market peak: November-January when supply is low

TOMATO - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kano, Kaduna, Plateau, Sokoto, Jigawa
Planting season: October-February (dry season with irrigation)
Soil type: Sandy loam, pH 6.0-6.8
Maturity period: 70-90 days
Common diseases: Fusarium wilt, Bacterial canker, Tomato mosaic virus, Blight
Pest management: Use copper fungicide for blight, insecticide for whitefly
Recommended varieties: Roma VF, Beske, UC82B, Tropimech
Fertilizer: NPK 20-10-10 at transplanting, calcium nitrate during fruiting
Irrigation: Drip irrigation recommended, water every 2-3 days in dry season
Yield potential: 15-25 tonnes per hectare
Market peak: May-July when dry season supply drops

CASSAVA - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Benue, Cross River, Oyo, Ogun, Delta, Anambra
Planting season: March-May (start of rainy season)
Soil type: Sandy loam, pH 5.5-6.5
Maturity period: 12-18 months
Common diseases: Cassava mosaic disease, Cassava bacterial blight, Anthracnose
Pest management: Mealybug controlled by biological agents, weed early
Recommended varieties: TMS 30572, NR 8082, TME 419, UMUCASS 36
Fertilizer: NPK 12-12-17 at 6 weeks after planting
Yield potential: 20-40 tonnes per hectare
Processing: Can be processed into garri, flour, starch, fufu
Market peak: December-March when demand for garri is highest

RICE - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kebbi, Niger, Anambra, Ebonyi, Benue, Taraba
Planting season: May-June (upland), June-July (lowland/irrigated)
Soil type: Clay loam for lowland, well-drained loam for upland, pH 5.5-6.5
Maturity period: 100-130 days
Common diseases: Rice blast, Brown spot, Bacterial leaf blight
Pest management: African rice gall midge controlled by early planting
Recommended varieties: FARO 44, FARO 52, NERICA 1, NERICA 4
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 basal, urea top dressing at tillering
Irrigation: Flood irrigation for lowland, supplemental for upland
Yield potential: 4-8 tonnes per hectare irrigated
Market peak: February-April before new harvest arrives

SORGHUM - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kano, Kaduna, Borno, Yobe, Sokoto, Zamfara
Planting season: June-July with onset of rains
Soil type: Well-drained loamy to clay soils, pH 5.5-7.5
Maturity period: 90-140 days
Common diseases: Grain mold, Striga weed (major problem), Anthracnose
Pest management: Use Striga-resistant varieties, rotate with cowpea
Recommended varieties: SAMSORG 14, SAMSORG 17, SK 5912
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at planting, urea at 4-6 weeks
Drought tolerance: High — suitable for low rainfall areas below 600mm
Yield potential: 2-4 tonnes per hectare
Uses: Food, animal feed, brewing, biofuel

COWPEA (BEANS) - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kano, Borno, Sokoto, Kebbi, Niger, Kaduna
Planting season: June-July (single rainy season), March (irrigation)
Soil type: Sandy loam, pH 6.0-7.0
Maturity period: 60-90 days
Common diseases: Cowpea mosaic virus, Septoria leaf spot, Root rot
Pest management: Thrips and pod borers — use lambda-cyhalothrin
Recommended varieties: IT97K-499-35, SAMPEA 10, SAMPEA 14
Fertilizer: Minimal nitrogen needed (fixes own nitrogen), apply phosphorus
Intercropping: Commonly intercropped with maize and sorghum
Yield potential: 0.8-2.5 tonnes per hectare
Market peak: October-December after harvest, prices rise January-June

GROUNDNUT (PEANUT) - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kano, Kaduna, Sokoto, Katsina, Zamfara, Kebbi
Planting season: May-June with first rains
Soil type: Sandy loam, well-drained, pH 5.9-7.0
Maturity period: 90-120 days
Common diseases: Early leaf spot, Late leaf spot, Rosette virus
Pest management: Treat seeds with fungicide before planting
Recommended varieties: SAMNUT 22, SAMNUT 23, SAMNUT 24
Fertilizer: Single superphosphate at planting, gypsum at pegging
Aflatoxin risk: Dry properly after harvest to avoid aflatoxin contamination
Yield potential: 1-2.5 tonnes per hectare
Uses: Oil production, groundnut cake, direct consumption

YAM - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Benue, Plateau, Niger, Taraba, Kogi, Enugu
Planting season: February-March (early planting for best yield)
Soil type: Deep well-drained loamy soil, pH 5.5-6.5
Maturity period: 7-8 months
Common diseases: Yam mosaic virus, Anthracnose, Dry rot
Pest management: Yam beetle controlled by treating setts before planting
Recommended varieties: Puna, Ife, TDr 89/02665, Kemi
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at 6 weeks, mulch to retain moisture
Storage: Store in well-ventilated barns, treat with wood ash
Yield potential: 15-30 tonnes per hectare
Market peak: December-May when old stock depletes before new harvest

PEPPER (CHILI) - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kaduna, Kano, Plateau, Oyo, Ogun
Planting season: September-October (nursery), November-December (transplant)
Soil type: Well-drained loamy soil, pH 6.0-6.8
Maturity period: 90-120 days after transplanting
Common diseases: Phytophthora blight, Pepper veinal mottle virus, Anthracnose
Pest management: Aphids and mites — use insecticidal soap or neem oil
Recommended varieties: Rodo (tatashe), Shombo, Bawa, Tatase
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at transplanting, foliar feed every 3 weeks
Irrigation: Regular watering especially during fruit development
Yield potential: 8-15 tonnes per hectare fresh weight
Market peak: July-September when demand peaks for festive cooking

ONION - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kebbi, Sokoto, Kano, Jigawa, Niger
Planting season: October-November (dry season with irrigation)
Soil type: Sandy loam, well-drained, pH 6.0-7.0
Maturity period: 90-120 days
Common diseases: Purple blotch, Downy mildew, Fusarium basal rot
Pest management: Thrips are major pest — use spinosad or lambda-cyhalothrin
Recommended varieties: Jar Maka, Bello, Dogon Yaro, Red Creole
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at planting, urea side dressing at 6 weeks
Storage: Cure in field for 2 weeks, store in cool dry ventilated place
Yield potential: 20-30 tonnes per hectare
Market peak: June-September when stored supply runs low
