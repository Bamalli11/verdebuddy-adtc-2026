MAIZE (CORN) - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kaduna, Kano, Plateau, Benue, Niger
Other states found: Nasarawa, Taraba, Kwara, Kogi, Oyo, Bauchi, Gombe, Adamawa
Planting season: March-April (first season), July-August (second season)
Soil type: Well-drained loamy soil, pH 5.5-7.0
Maturity period: 90-120 days
Recommended varieties: SAMMAZ 15, SAMMAZ 27, OBA SUPER 2, TZPB SR
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at planting, urea top-dressing at 6 weeks (150 kg/ha)
Spacing: 75cm between rows, 25cm within rows, 1 seed per hole
Planting guide: Till soil to 15-20cm depth. Plant seeds 3-5cm deep. Thin to one seedling per stand at 2 weeks. Apply herbicide within 3 days of planting. Weed at 3 and 6 weeks. Apply urea when plants are knee-high.
Common diseases: Maize streak virus (yellow streaks on leaves), Northern leaf blight (long grey-green lesions), Gray leaf spot
Pest management: Use IPM approach. For stem borers apply carbofuran at base of plant. Neem extract for aphids. Scout weekly.
Yield potential: 3-5 tonnes/ha (improved varieties up to 7 tonnes with good management)
Market peak: November-January
Profit per hectare: N600,000 - N1,200,000
Input cost estimate: N250,000 - N450,000
ROI classification: Medium
Storage life: 6-12 months (dry grain at 12-13% moisture)
Export demand score: 5/10
Climate sensitivity: 500-1200mm rainfall needed

TOMATO - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kano, Kaduna, Plateau, Sokoto, Jigawa
Other states found: Katsina, Kebbi, Zamfara, Nasarawa, FCT, Ogun, Osun, Oyo
Planting season: October-February (irrigated dry season), May-June (rainy season)
Soil type: Sandy loam, pH 6.0-6.8
Maturity period: 70-90 days
Recommended varieties: Roma VF, UC82B, Tropimech, Beske, Thorgal
Fertilizer: NPK 20-10-10 at transplanting, calcium nitrate weekly foliar spray
Spacing: 60cm between rows, 45cm within rows
Planting guide: Raise seedlings in nursery for 4 weeks. Transplant in the evening or on a cloudy day. Irrigate immediately after transplanting. Stake plants when 30cm tall. Prune suckers on indeterminate varieties. Apply fertilizer in split doses every 2 weeks.
Common diseases: Fusarium wilt, Bacterial canker, Early and late blight
Pest management: Copper fungicide for fungal diseases. Imidacloprid for whiteflies. Use yellow sticky traps.
Yield potential: 15-25 tonnes/ha
Market peak: May-July
Profit per hectare: N1,500,000 - N3,500,000
Input cost estimate: N800,000 - N1,500,000
ROI classification: High
Storage life: 1-3 weeks fresh, 12 months processed
Export demand score: 6/10
Climate sensitivity: 600-1000mm

Other states found: Ondo, Edo, Rivers, Bayelsa, Imo, Abia, Ebonyi, Kwara, Kogi, Osun, Ekiti

Maturity period: 12-18 months
Recommended varieties: TME 419, TMS 98/0505, UMUCASS 36, IITA-TMS-I011368
Fertilizer: NPK 12-12-17 at 2 months, 120 kg/ha
Spacing: 1m x 1m for table varieties, 0.8m x 0.8m for processing

Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at transplanting (200 kg/ha), urea top-dress at tillering (100 kg/ha)

Yield potential: 2-4 tonnes/ha (rain-fed), 4-8 tonnes/ha (irrigated)

Planting season: February-March (early), April (late)

Maturity period: 7-8 months (white yam), 5-6 months (water yam)
Recommended varieties: Puna, Ife, Amula, Pepa
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at 100 kg/ha at 6-8 weeks
Spacing: 1m x 1m with stakes or mounds
Planting guide: Plant setts of 200-400g. Treat with wood ash and fungicide before planting. Create mounds 30cm high. Stakes are essential. Weed at 6 and 12 weeks. Harvest when vines turn yellow.
Common diseases: Yam Mosaic Virus, Anthracnose, Dry rot
Pest management: Treat setts before planting. Control weevils with carbofuran. Rotate with cowpea or groundnut.
Yield potential: 15-30 tonnes/ha

Input cost estimate: N800,000 - N1,500,000

GINGER - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Kaduna, Nasarawa, Plateau
Other states found: Taraba, Bauchi, Gombe, Adamawa, Benue, Niger, Kogi, Enugu
Planting season: April-May
Soil type: Well-drained sandy loam, rich organic matter
Maturity period: 8-10 months
Recommended varieties: UG1, UG2 (Tafin giwa local)
Fertilizer: Organic compost 10 tonnes/ha plus NPK 15-15-15 200 kg/ha in 3 split doses
Spacing: 30cm x 30cm in ridges
Planting guide: Select healthy disease-free rhizomes. Soak in fungicide overnight before planting. Plant 5cm deep. Mulch heavily. Water twice weekly in dry spells. Harvest when leaves turn yellow at 8-10 months.
Common diseases: Rhizome rot (Pythium - most serious), Bacterial wilt. Avoid waterlogging.
Post-harvest: Wash, dry, and grade for export. Fresh ginger commands premium October-February.
Yield potential: 15-30 tonnes/ha fresh
Market peak: September-February (export)
Profit per hectare: N2,000,000 - N5,000,000
Input cost estimate: N700,000 - N1,500,000
ROI classification: Very High
Storage life: 4-6 months fresh, 12+ months dried
Export demand score: 10/10 (top Nigerian export crop)

COCOA - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Ondo, Cross River, Ogun, Osun, Ekiti
Other states found: Edo, Delta, Kwara, Kogi, Abia, Anambra, Rivers, Imo
Planting season: May-June (start of rains)
Soil type: Deep loamy soil with good drainage and organic matter
Maturity period: 3-5 years to first harvest, 8-10 years peak production
Recommended varieties: CRIN hybrids, Amazon Upper
Fertilizer: Muriate of potash plus urea annually from year 2
Spacing: 3m x 3m with shade trees
Planting guide: Dig holes 60cm x 60cm x 60cm. Provide shade with plantain for first 2 years. Prune to single trunk to 1.5m then allow 3-4 main branches. Remove chupons regularly. Harvest pods when yellow or orange.
Common diseases: Black pod (Phytophthora - 30-50% loss), Cocoa swollen shoot virus, Vascular streak dieback
Pest management: Capsids - lambda-cyhalothrin. Black pod - copper fungicide every 3 weeks in rains.
Fermentation and drying: Ferment beans 5-7 days. Sun dry to 7.5% moisture.
Yield potential: 300-800 kg/ha dry beans
Market peak: October-March
Profit per hectare: N800,000 - N2,500,000
Input cost estimate: N500,000 - N1,000,000
ROI classification: Very High
Storage life: 6-12 months properly dried
Export demand score: 10/10 (Nigeria is world 4th largest producer)

IRISH POTATO - NIGERIA
Best planting states: Plateau (Jos), Kaduna, Benue highland areas
Other states found: Taraba highlands, Adamawa highlands, Bauchi, Gombe highlands, FCT
Planting season: March-May (rainy), August-September (dry season highlands)
Soil type: Well-drained loamy soil, pH 5.0-6.0
Maturity period: 90-120 days
Recommended varieties: Nicola, Diamant, Rosita, Atica
Fertilizer: NPK 15-15-15 at 400 kg/ha, muriate of potash at earthing-up
Spacing: 75cm between rows, 30cm within rows with earthing-up required
Planting guide: Chit seed potatoes for 2 weeks. Cut tubers to 30-50g pieces with at least 2 eyes. Plant 8-10cm deep. Earth up at 4 and 8 weeks. Harvest when haulms die back.

GENERAL PLANTING GUIDE FOR ALL CROPS

Soil preparation:
Plough to 20-30cm depth at least 2 weeks before planting. For clay soils add sand and organic matter to improve drainage. For sandy soils add compost to improve water retention. Take soil test every 3 years from State Ministry of Agriculture.

Seed selection:
Always use certified seeds from NASC or state agricultural agencies. Avoid saving seeds from hybrid varieties. Treat seeds with fungicide and insecticide before planting. Test germination with 10 seeds on wet cotton - at least 8 should sprout.

Water management:
Most crops need 25-50mm water per week. Water in morning to reduce evaporation and disease. Drip irrigation saves 40-60% water. Waterlogging kills more crops than drought.

Weed control:
Critical in first 6 weeks. Use herbicides only on labeled crops. Mulching reduces weeding by 50-70%. Never let weeds flower and set seed.

Fertilizer application:
Split into 2-3 doses. Apply when soil is moist. Keep fertilizer away from stem to avoid burning.

Pest and disease management:
Scout fields twice weekly. Treat at economic threshold. Rotate crops every season. Keep field records.

Harvest and post-harvest:
Harvest at right maturity in cool morning hours. Grade and sort before market. Use hermetic bags or metal silos for grain storage.
