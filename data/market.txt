NIGERIA AGRICULTURAL MARKET PRICES AND ADVISORY

COMMODITY PRICES BY SEASON (Reference prices in Naira — verify locally as prices change)

MAIZE (PER 100KG BAG)
Post-harvest (October-December): N18,000 - N25,000
Lean season (April-August): N35,000 - N55,000
Average price: N30,000 - N40,000
Price trend: Rising due to fuel costs and naira depreciation
Best time to sell: April-July for maximum profit
Best markets: Dawanau market Kano, Mile 12 Lagos, Gboko Benue
Storage tip: Store in hermetic bags to sell during lean season
Processing option: Sell as flour for higher value — N500-700 per kg

RICE (PER 50KG BAG LOCAL)
Post-harvest (November-January): N35,000 - N45,000
Lean season (June-September): N55,000 - N75,000
Average price: N45,000 - N60,000
Imported rice price: N65,000 - N80,000 per 50kg
Best time to sell: June-August when imports are expensive
Best markets: Kebbi state markets, Abuja markets, Lagos
Processing option: Mill and brand locally — commands premium price
Value addition: Parboiling increases shelf life and price by 20-30%

CASSAVA (FRESH ROOTS PER TONNE)
Harvest season: N30,000 - N50,000 per tonne
Off-season: N60,000 - N90,000 per tonne
Garri (processed): N400-700 per kg
Cassava flour: N350-500 per kg
Starch: N300-450 per kg
Best markets: Iwo Road Ibadan, Eke Awka, Onitsha main market
Processing advice: Never sell raw cassava if you can process — profit doubles
Garri processing: 4 tonnes fresh cassava = 1 tonne garri approximately

TOMATO (PER 50KG BASKET)
Peak harvest (November-January north): N3,000 - N8,000
Lean season (May-September): N25,000 - N60,000
Price volatility: Highest of any crop — can change weekly
Best markets: Kano tomato market, Mile 12 Lagos
Storage problem: Tomato has 3-5 day shelf life after harvest
Solution: Sell to processors or dry into tomato powder
Tomato powder value: N2,000-3,500 per kg — very profitable
Advice: Never plant tomato without knowing your buyer in advance

ONION (PER 100 BULBS OR 50KG BAG)
Post-harvest (February-April): N15,000 - N25,000
Lean season (August-November): N40,000 - N80,000
Best markets: Dawanau Kano, Bodija Ibadan, Mile 12 Lagos
Storage: Onion stores 4-6 months if properly cured and ventilated
Storage advice: Hang in nets in cool dry place — do not pile on floor
Export opportunity: Niger, Chad, Cameroon buy Nigerian onion
Price record: Onion hit N120,000 per bag in 2023 lean season

GROUNDNUT (PER 100KG BAG SHELLED)
Post-harvest (November-January): N120,000 - N160,000
Lean season (June-September): N200,000 - N280,000
Groundnut oil: N2,500-4,000 per litre
Groundnut cake: N400-600 per kg
Best markets: Kano, Kaduna, Sokoto
Processing advice: Oil extraction doubles revenue from raw nuts
Export potential: High — groundnut oil exports to West Africa

COWPEA/BEANS (PER 100KG BAG)
Post-harvest (October-December): N90,000 - N130,000
Lean season (April-August): N180,000 - N260,000
Best markets: Dawanau Kano, Bodija Ibadan, Onitsha
Storage: Stores well for 6-12 months in hermetic bags with ash
Advice: Hermetic storage during harvest season, sell in lean season
Value addition: Processed bean flour (for moi-moi) N800-1200 per kg

YAM (PER TUBER OR 100KG)
New yam (August-September): N500-1,500 per tuber
Lean season (April-July): N2,000-5,000 per tuber
Per 100kg: N25,000-80,000 depending on season
Best markets: Makurdi, Otukpo, Gboko in Benue, Onitsha
New yam festival: Prices peak just before festivals in August-September
Export: Yam is Nigeria top agricultural export — UK, USA markets pay premium
Advice: Grade and size your yam — large tubers command double price

SORGHUM (PER 100KG BAG)
Post-harvest (November-January): N25,000 - N35,000
Lean season (June-September): N45,000 - N65,000
Best markets: Kano, Maiduguri, Yola, Sokoto
Industrial buyers: Breweries (for sorghum beer), feed mills
Contract farming: Nigerian Breweries and ABInBev buy directly from farmers
Advice: Register with NAFDAC-approved buyers for better prices

PEPPER/CHILI (PER KG DRIED)
Fresh pepper per kg: N800-2,500
Dried chili per kg: N2,000-5,000
Ground pepper per kg: N3,500-7,000
Best markets: Bodija Ibadan, Yam market Onitsha, Sabon Gari Kano
Drying: Sun dry on raised platforms — do not dry on ground
Export potential: Very high — Europe and US buy Nigerian dried pepper
Value addition: Grinding and packaging increases value by 50-100%

MARKET ADVISORY TIPS

SELLING STRATEGIES
1. Never sell immediately after harvest — prices are always lowest
2. Store for at least 2-3 months if you can afford to wait
3. Join a cooperative to sell in bulk and negotiate better prices
4. Know the price before harvesting — call buyers in advance
5. Grade your produce — better quality always gets better price
6. Reduce post-harvest losses — they eat into your profit more than low prices

POST-HARVEST LOSS PREVENTION
Maize and grains: Use hermetic bags (e.g. Purdue Improved Crop Storage bags)
Tomato and vegetables: Sell within 3 days or process immediately
Yam: Store in well-ventilated barns, treat with wood ash
Cassava: Process within 24-48 hours of harvest to avoid rotting
Onion: Cure in field for 2 weeks, store hung in nets
