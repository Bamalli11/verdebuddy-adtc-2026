MAJOR AGRICULTURAL MARKETS IN NIGERIA

DAWANAU GRAIN MARKET - KANO
Location: Dawanau, Kano State
Type: Wholesale grain market - largest in West Africa
Products: Maize, millet, sorghum, cowpea, groundnut, sesame, rice, wheat

Typical prices (per 100kg bag, 2026):
- Maize: N45,000 - N70,000 (low after harvest Oct-Dec, high June-Sept)
- Millet: N50,000 - N80,000
- Sorghum: N40,000 - N60,000
- Cowpea (white): N100,000 - N180,000
- Groundnut (shelled): N150,000 - N220,000
- Sesame: N200,000 - N320,000
- Rice (local milled): N80,000 - N130,000

MARKET PRICE SEASONALITY GUIDE

Understanding when prices are high and low is key to profit.

MAIZE PRICES
Lowest: October-January (just after harvest, everyone selling)
Highest: July-September (before harvest, old stocks depleted)
Strategy: Buy or store in October, sell in July. 40-60% price difference possible.
Storage tip: Hermetic bag + 1 tablet Phostoxin per 100kg bag = safe storage 9-12 months

COWPEA PRICES
Lowest: November-January (post harvest)
Highest: May-July (pre harvest)
Strategy: Same as maize. Storage in hermetic bags critical - weevils destroy cowpea fast.

TOMATO PRICES
Lowest: October-December (harvest season north Nigeria)
Highest: February-April (supply from north has ended)
Price swing: Can be 300% difference between low and high
Investment tip: Tomato processing (paste/puree) captures value when farm price is low.

ONION PRICES
Lowest: December-February (harvest and dry storage)
Highest: August-September (end of storage season)
Strategy: Buy in December, sell in August. Onions store 4-6 months when properly cured.

CASSAVA/GARRI PRICES
Lowest: December-February (harvest season in south)
Highest: June-September (long rains reduce supply)
Strategy: Process cassava into garri during harvest (price low). Store garri. Sell before next harvest.

YAMS
Lowest: August-October (new yam season)
Highest: February-May (old yam nearly exhausted, new not yet ready)
New yam festival: Triggers demand spike in August-September in Igbo/Yoruba areas.

VALUE ADDITION OPPORTUNITIES

VALUE ADDITION = Processing raw farm products into higher-value goods

CASSAVA VALUE CHAIN
Raw cassava (N80,000/tonne) -> Garri (N400,000/tonne equivalent) = 5x value
Raw cassava (N80,000/tonne) -> Cassava flour HQCF (N500,000/tonne) = 6x value
Raw cassava (N80,000/tonne) -> Starch (N700,000/tonne) = 9x value
Investment needed: Small garri processor N500,000. Medium N5,000,000. Large factory N50,000,000+

TOMATO VALUE CHAIN
Fresh tomato (N20,000/50kg basket when cheap) -> Tomato paste (N150,000/50 tins) = 7x value
Investment: Small processor N2,000,000. Commercial N50,000,000+
Market: Hotels, restaurants, households all buy paste. Export to diaspora markets.

GROUNDNUT VALUE CHAIN
Raw groundnut (N150,000/100kg) -> Groundnut oil (N250,000 from 100kg) + cake (N50,000) = 2x+
Investment: Cold press oil machine N300,000-N1,000,000
Market: Cooking oil, cosmetics, animal feed (cake)

CASHEW VALUE CHAIN
Raw cashew nut RCN (N700,000/tonne) -> Processed cashew kernel (N2,500,000/tonne) = 3.5x value
Investment: Small processing plant N5,000,000. Large N100,000,000+
Market: EU supermarkets, Asia, USA. Strong demand growing.

GINGER VALUE CHAIN
Fresh ginger (N200,000/tonne farm gate) -> Dried ginger (N1,500,000/tonne) = 7.5x value
Fresh ginger (N200,000/tonne) -> Ginger powder (N2,500,000/tonne) = 12.5x value
Investment: Dryer N500,000-N2,000,000. Grinder N300,000-N800,000.

SESAME VALUE CHAIN
Raw sesame (N800,000/tonne) -> Sesame oil (N2,000,000 from 1 tonne) = 2.5x value
Raw sesame (N800,000/tonne) -> Tahini paste (N2,500,000/tonne) = 3x value
Investment: Roaster + expeller N1,500,000-N5,000,000

CATFISH VALUE CHAIN
Live catfish (N3,000/kg farm gate) -> Smoked catfish (N5,500/kg) = 1.8x value
Live catfish (N3,000/kg) -> Catfish fillet frozen (N4,500/kg) = 1.5x value
Investment: Smoking kiln N200,000-N500,000. Cold storage N1,000,000-N5,000,000.

EXPORT MARKET GUIDE FOR NIGERIAN AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS

TOP EXPORT DESTINATIONS AND REQUIREMENTS

EUROPEAN UNION (EU)
Entry requirements: GlobalGAP certification, EU Maximum Residue Limits (MRL) compliance, phytosanitary certificate
Top Nigerian products accepted: Sesame, hibiscus, cashew kernel, ginger, green beans, avocado
Products banned/restricted: Many fresh vegetables due to MRL failures (pesticide residues)
How to comply: Stop pesticide use 2 weeks before harvest. Use EU-approved pesticides only. Apply for GlobalGAP (cost N300,000-N1,000,000 with consultant).

UNITED STATES (USA)
Entry requirements: FDA registration, phytosanitary certificate, USDA compliance
Top products: Cocoa, sesame, hibiscus, cashew, palm kernel
Opportunity: Nigerian diaspora market (3+ million) buys garri, egusi, dried crayfish, achi, uziza at premium price.

CHINA
Top imports from Nigeria: Sesame (largest buyer), cashew, cocoa butter, palm kernel
Entry: Standard phytosanitary. No special certification required for most products.
Price: China often offers best price for bulk sesame.

INDIA
Top imports from Nigeria: Cashew raw nuts (largest single importer), sesame, cocoa
Entry: Standard process.

MIDDLE EAST (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait)
Top imports: Sesame, ginger, hibiscus, dried pepper
Special: Halal certification needed for processed food items.
Opportunity: Large Nigerian/West African diaspora in UAE creates demand for local food products.

UNITED KINGDOM
Post-Brexit: UK now has independent standards. Similar to EU requirements.
Strong diaspora demand for: Garri, yam, plantain, jollof rice ingredients, egusi.
UK supermarket chains: Waitrose, Marks & Spencer buying Nigerian cashew, hibiscus.

EXPORT PROCESS STEP BY STEP

Step 1: Register business with CAC (Corporate Affairs Commission) - N10,000-N50,000
Step 2: Register with NEPC (Nigerian Export Promotion Council) - N50,000
Step 3: Open domiciliary (USD) account with a commercial bank
Step 4: Get NAQS phytosanitary certificate for each shipment - N5,000-N50,000
Step 5: Get NAFDAC certification if exporting processed products - N50,000-N500,000
Step 6: Find buyer (trade fairs, online B2B platforms, export agents)
Step 7: Book container with shipping line (CMA CGM, Maersk, MSC - present at Lagos/Apapa)
Step 8: Clear customs with customs agent/broker
Step 9: Receive payment (L/C - Letter of Credit is safest for first-time exports)

Useful contacts:
- NEPC (Nigerian Export Promotion Council): export@nepc.gov.ng | 01-7957133
- NAQS (Agricultural Quarantine): naqs@yahoo.com
- NAFDAC: info@nafdac.gov.ng
- Lagos Chamber of Commerce: info@lcci.org.ng

AGRICULTURAL FINANCE AND CREDIT

BANK OF AGRICULTURE (BOA)
Products: Small farmer loans N50,000-N5,000,000 at 9% interest
Requirements: BVN, land document, farm plan
Contact: BOA Head Office, Abuja +234 9 2900076

CBN ANCHOR BORROWERS PROGRAMME
For: Rice, wheat, maize, cassava, tomato farmers
Interest rate: 9% per annum
How to access: Through registered commodity associations or cooperatives
Contact: Any commercial bank with agricultural desk

NIRSAL AGRI-GUARANTEE
De-risks loans from commercial banks
Makes agricultural lending less risky for banks
Farmers can access commercial bank loans at lower rate with NIRSAL guarantee

AFEX COMMODITY FINANCE
Warehouse receipt financing
Deposit grain in AFEX warehouse, get up to 70% of value as loan
Repay when you sell the grain
Benefit: Do not have to sell immediately at harvest low price
Contact: AFEX Commodities Exchange, Lagos/Kano

COOPERATIVE SOCIETY LOANS
Join or form cooperative with minimum 20 members
Access state government and bank agricultural loans collectively
Interest rates often lower than individual loans
State ADP (Agricultural Development Programme) supports cooperative formation
